Primary Question:

My dissertation explores pre- and early-modern Sufi poetry in Punjabi, especially the oeuvre of Bulleh Shah (1680-1757), in order to discover tools for spiritual and social healing for contemporary societies.

Areas of focus:

  • Poetry as Pedagogy: how the ritual of repetition and other elements of poetic form inculcate bodily forms of knowing
  • Memory and Authorship: the relationship of living communities with the poetic personae and the works attributed to them
  • Aesthetics of Language: the role of orality and the relationship between Punjabi language and culture

Tentative Conclusions:

  • Knowing is relational
  • Knowing is experiential and embodied (knowing vs. knowledge)
  • Knowing has both analytical and synthetic elements
